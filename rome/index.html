How to play

A fan adaptation of the classic city-building card game, with a curated set of buildings. Every card is three things at once: a role to lead, a material to build with, and a building to construct.

On your turn either THINK (refill your hand, or draw one, or take a Jack — a wild role card) or LEAD a role by playing a card. Everyone else may follow (play a matching card) or think. Everyone who joined performs the action — once themselves, plus once per matching client. Played cards land in the shared pool.

The six roles (each tied to a material):

  • LABORER (rubble) — take a pool card into your stockpile.
  • PATRON (marble) — hire a pool card as a client. Clients act with you every time their role runs.
  • CRAFTSMAN (wood) — start a building on a matching site, or add a matching material from your hand.
  • ARCHITECT (concrete) — like the craftsman, but builds with materials from your stockpile.
  • LEGIONARY (brick) — demand a material: take it from the pool and from every rival's hand.
  • MERCHANT (stone) — bank a stockpile material in your vault (points at game end).

Buildings complete when they hold materials equal to their value (rubble/wood 1, brick/concrete 2, stone/marble 3). Completing one grants that much influence — which raises your clientele and vault limits — and switches on the building's power. The powers are where the game lives: read them on your hand cards.

Game end: the deck runs dry or the building sites do; highest influence + vault wins. Complete the FORUM with a client of every role and you win on the spot.

Simplifications vs the tabletop original: a trimmed building list, no out-of-town sites, no petitions, and no merchant majority bonus. Cass and Boone play a solid economic game — watch your hand when they lead the legion. Good luck out there, dweller.
