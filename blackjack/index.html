How to play blackjack

The goal: beat the dealer by getting closer to 21 without going over (“busting”). You only play against the dealer — never against the other players at the table.

Card values: number cards count as printed, face cards (J, Q, K) count 10, and an ace counts 1 or 11, whichever helps you more (a hand using an ace as 11 is called “soft”).

A round: place your bet, then everyone gets two cards; the dealer gets one face up and one hidden. Each player acts in turn, then the dealer reveals the hidden card and must keep drawing until reaching 17. Finish closer to 21 than the dealer — or watch the dealer bust — and you win.

Your options on a turn:

  • Hit — take another card. Keep hitting as long as you like, but bust and the bet is gone.
  • Stand — keep what you have and end your turn.
  • Double — double your bet and take exactly one more card. Strongest when your first two cards total 10 or 11.
  • Split — when your two cards match, split them into two separate hands (adds a second bet equal to your first).
  • Surrender — fold your first two cards and keep half your bet. Only worth it against a very strong dealer card.
  • Insurance — a side bet offered when the dealer shows an ace. It pays 2:1 if the dealer has blackjack, but it loses money in the long run — the correct play is to always skip it.

Payouts: a win pays even money, a “blackjack” (ace + ten-value as your first two cards) pays 3:2, and a tie (“push”) returns your bet.
