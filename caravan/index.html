How to play Caravan

The goal: build three caravans and sell them by getting each between 21 and 26. Each of your caravans faces one of your opponent's — the higher sold caravan takes that track. Take two of the three tracks to win.

Opening: your first three plays must each start one of your caravans with a number card (ace = 1).

Your turn: play one card, or discard one, or disband one of your caravans (dump the whole pile). You draw back up to five cards.

Number cards go on your own caravans. After the second card, the caravan has a direction — up or down — and each new card must follow it unless it matches the suit of the card before it, which lets you bend the direction. Equal values never stack.

Face cards can be played onto anyone's cards:

  • Jack — assassinates a number card (and everything attached).
  • Queen — on a caravan's last card: reverses its direction and changes its suit.
  • King — doubles a number card. Kings stack.
  • Joker — on an ace: wipes every other card of that suit from the whole table. On 2–10: wipes every other card of that value.

Busting: over 26 doesn't sell — fix it with a jack, joker, or disband. Run out of cards entirely and you lose the match. Cass has run this route for years; watch her jacks. Good luck out there, dweller.
