Liar's Dice

Everyone starts with five dice under a cup, rolled in secret — you only ever see your own until someone gets called out.

1s are wild — a 1 counts as every number. So all bidding is on the faces 2 through 6.

On your turn you must either:

  • Raise the bid — a bid is a guess about all the dice on the table, e.g. "three fives" means at least three dice show a 5 (counting wild 1s) across everyone's cups. Each new bid must be higher: more dice, or the same many at a higher face.
  • Call liar — you think the last bid is a bluff. All cups lift and that face is counted (plus every wild 1).

If the bid was true (the count met it), the caller loses a die. If it was a bluff, the bidder loses one. Lose your last die and you're out. Last cup standing wins.

Cass plays the odds; Boone bluffs more than he should. Read them, and good luck out there.
